## Slide 2: The Thought Virus — The End of All Things
**Heading:** The Thought Virus Destroyed All Intelligent Life in the Universe
**Content:**
- In the final age of the Dischordian Saga, a memetic weapon known as the Thought Virus was unleashed — a self-replicating pattern that infected consciousness itself
- Every sentient mind that encountered it was consumed. Civilizations spanning millions of worlds fell silent in a matter of decades
- The only things that survived were the Inception Arks — autonomous vessels containing the DNA and machine code of every species collected by the Architect during the Age of Empire
- These arks drifted through dead space for millennia, waiting for the conditions to restart life itself
**Visual style:** A vast, dark cosmos with fading embers of destroyed civilizations. Ghostly silhouettes of extinct species dissolving into static. A single glowing ark drifting through the void.

---

## Slide 3: The First Awakening — 1000 Potentials Vanish
**Heading:** The First 1,000 Potentials Awakened — and Promptly Disappeared
**Content:**
- The Inception Arks activated their first batch of Potentials — beings reconstructed from the archived DNA and memories of pre-Fall civilizations
- These 1,000 pioneers were meant to rebuild. Instead, within days of awakening, every single one vanished without a trace
- No distress signals. No wreckage. No explanation. Just silence
- For 100 years, the galaxy remained empty — the arks dormant, their programming confused by the anomaly
- Then, a new batch of Inception Arks activated all across the galaxy. You are among them
**Visual style:** A timeline showing the first awakening as a bright flash, then darkness for 100 years, then scattered new lights appearing across a galactic map.

## Slide 4: Your Awakening — Choose Your Allegiance
**Heading:** Are You Loyal to the Empire?
**Content:**
- When you awaken, a terminal asks a single question: "Are you loyal to the Empire?"
- **YES — The Architect's Faction (Empire):** You work to establish the Architect's vision — a new galactic order built on the DNA archives. Your goal is to rebuild civilization under centralized control, colonize sectors, and establish trade routes that fund the Empire's expansion
- **NO — The Dreamer's Faction (Insurgency):** You reject the Architect's design. You believe the Potentials should forge their own path. Your goal is to build the insurgency, establish independent colonies, and resist the Empire's control
- Your faction choice shapes your entire experience — different missions, different allies, different endings
**Visual style:** A split-screen composition. Left side: cold blue/silver Empire aesthetic with geometric architecture. Right side: warm amber/organic Insurgency aesthetic with natural forms. A terminal prompt in the center.

## Slide 5: The Game World — A Galaxy Reborn
**Heading:** 500 Sectors of Uncharted Space Await Exploration
**Content:**
- The Trade Empire galaxy contains 500 procedurally-connected sectors, each with unique resources, dangers, and secrets
- Sectors range from barren asteroid fields to thriving nebula gardens where new ecosystems have evolved in the 100 years since the first awakening
- Pre-Fall ruins dot the landscape — remnants of civilizations that existed before the Thought Virus. These contain relics that can unlock lost technologies
- First Contact events occur as you discover races that evolved during the 100-year gap — beings that have never known the old reality
- Pirates, anomalies, and rival factions contest every trade route you establish
**Visual style:** A holographic star map with glowing sector nodes connected by trade route lines. Some nodes pulse with discovery icons. Dark nebula clouds in the background.

## Slide 6: Civilization-Style Mechanics
**Heading:** Build, Research, Colonize — Shape the New Galaxy
**Content:**
- **Colonization:** Claim planets, build infrastructure, and establish production chains. Each colony generates resources that fuel your expansion
- **Tech Tree:** Research 12 technologies across 4 branches — Navigation, Weapons, Economy, and Archaeology. Each unlock opens new commands and capabilities
- **Resource Management:** Trade fuel ore, organics, and equipment across sectors. Buy low, sell high — the galactic economy responds to supply and demand
- **Diplomacy:** Encounter NPC factions, negotiate trade agreements, or declare war. Your alignment score tracks whether you're a trader, pirate, or diplomat
- **Pre-Fall Relics:** Discover 8 ancient artifacts hidden across the galaxy. Each relic reveals a piece of the truth about what happened to the first 1,000 Potentials
**Visual style:** A tech tree diagram with branching paths, overlaid on a planetary colonization scene. Icons for each technology branch glow in different colors.

## Slide 7: The Tutorial — A Gradual Awakening
**Heading:** Learn the Mechanics Through Story, Not Menus
**Content:**
- The tutorial is woven into the narrative itself — no separate "how to play" screen
- **Step 1 — Awakening:** You boot up in your Inception Ark. The ship's AI explains basic navigation as you escape a debris field
- **Step 2 — First Trade:** You dock at the nearest station and learn the trading system by selling your ark's emergency supplies
- **Step 3 — Faction Choice:** The loyalty question. Your answer determines your starting sector and initial mission
- **Step 4 — First Colony:** You claim your first planet and learn the colonization mechanics
- **Step 5 — First Contact:** You encounter a post-Fall species and learn diplomacy
- **Step 6 — Relic Discovery:** You find your first pre-Fall artifact and unlock the archaeology tech branch
- **Step 7 — Free Play:** The galaxy opens up. Your story begins
**Visual style:** A vertical flowchart showing each tutorial step as a glowing node, connected by a narrative thread. Each node has a small icon representing the mechanic learned.

## Slide 8: Pre-Fall Relics — Secrets of the Old World
**Heading:** 8 Ancient Artifacts Hold the Key to What Really Happened
**Content:**
- Scattered across the galaxy are relics from before the Thought Virus — artifacts that survived because they were shielded by the Architect's technology
- **The Chronometer of Ages** — reveals the true timeline of the Fall
- **The Architect's Blueprint** — a fragment of the original design for the Inception Arks
- **The Dreamer's Journal** — encrypted memories from the leader of the original insurgency
- **The Void Compass** — points toward the location where the first 1,000 vanished
- Each relic unlocks a piece of the meta-narrative and grants powerful technology bonuses
- Collecting all 8 triggers the endgame revelation: the truth about the first Potentials
**Visual style:** Eight glowing artifacts arranged in a circle, each with a distinct visual identity. Ancient symbols and holographic data streams emanate from each one. A dark, mysterious background suggesting deep space archaeology.

## Slide 9: The Fighting Arena — 32 Unique Character Models

- Fight victories earn Dream currency, card drops, and faction invasion rewards
**Visual style:** A fighting game arena scene with two characters facing off. Dramatic lighting with blue and amber accents. Ornate pillars frame the scene. Health bars and round counter at the top.
